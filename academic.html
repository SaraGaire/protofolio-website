Current Research Focus

Exploring the intersection of classical mechanics and computational mathematics

Chaplygin Ball Theory & Numerical Modeling

My primary research focuses on the mathematical modeling and computational analysis of the Chaplygin ball - a classical mechanics problem involving a sphere rolling inside a fixed sphere. This work combines theoretical physics with advanced numerical methods to understand complex dynamical systems.

Educational Philosophy

My approach to teaching centers on the belief that complex mathematical concepts become accessible when presented through interactive visualization and real-world applications. I strive to bridge the gap between theoretical understanding and practical implementation.

Core Principles

  • Connecting theory to practical applications
  • Encouraging collaborative problem-solving
  • Adapting to diverse learning styles

Teaching Methods
